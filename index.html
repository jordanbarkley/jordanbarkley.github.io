Super Smash Bros. Hacks (MIPS)

I am the founder of Super Smash Bros. 64 reverse engineering. I've introduced several new features such as additional stages and accompanying stage select screens, new characters, and an option menu for tournament and quality of life toggles. The original hack, 19XX, has been superseded by Smash Remix which has notably been featured in Kotaku gaming magazine and acquired millions of channel views. These hacks are primarily written in MIPS assembly with byuu's bass assembler.

This Super Smash Bros. 64 hack is a working proof of concept that the menu could be rewritten in C. The menu simply sets a few global structs before calling a startMatch() function. It's development was halted in favor of keeping the original feel of the game and expanding the original menu.

SePRO Lab Application Platfrom

I developed an iOS app to submit water sample metadata digitally for more precise data collection while also giving the lab time to prepare tests for incoming samples. The app was integrated with an existing Core LIMS Informatics Database via a RESTful API which allows customers to pull all past and future test results regardless if they were submitted with the app or not. Libraries/technologies used: Material Components for iOS, AlamoFire API, SwiftyJSON, Core LIMS Informatics Database, Location Services, Barcode Scanner

Weat

Weat allows users to suggest restaurants to their friends directly and generates suggestions to groups of friends using a kNN algorithm based on restaurants liked/disliked, date eaten, cost etc. It uses Swift/XIBs for it’s frontend and a mySQL database, Express server, and Facebook authentication for it’s backend

Tecmo Bowl Hacks

Originally developed as a father's day gift, this hack features custom graphics, all 32 NFL teams, and reimplementation of sounds removed to preserve space. This is accomplished by increasing the frequency of all sounds and then slowing the playback speed. A simple but elegant solution to a problem once considered unfixable due to NES hardware limitations

Ninja Gaiden Trilogy Hack

With the addition of the X and Y buttons to the SNES controller, many players find themselves changing their grip. When playing ports of NES games, muscle memory for the A and B buttons may fail depending on where the player rests their thumbs naturally. Nintendo addresses this issue in Super Mario Bros. All-Stars with the addition of “Type A” and “Type B” controls. This hack allows the player to toggle between “Type A” and “Type B” by pressing L or R respectively at any point in the game.
